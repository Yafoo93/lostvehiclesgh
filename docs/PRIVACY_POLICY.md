**LostVehiclesGH**

**Effective Date:** May 9, 2026

## 1. Introduction

LostVehiclesGH (“the Platform”, “we”, “our”, or “us”) is a Ghana-based vehicle recovery and verification platform operated by Kastech Inc., a registered technology company headquartered in Tamale, Ghana.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, disclose, and protect personal data when users access or use the LostVehiclesGH platform and related services.

LostVehiclesGH enables:

* vehicle registration and management,
* stolen or missing vehicle reporting,
* public vehicle status verification,
* recovery and sighting reporting workflows,
* moderation and fraud review processes,
* partner and law-enforcement collaboration tools.

We are committed to protecting user privacy and processing data responsibly in accordance with applicable laws, including the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843) of Ghana and internationally recognized privacy principles.

## 5. Public Search and Disclosure

LostVehiclesGH does not operate as a public vehicle directory.

Vehicle information is only displayed when a user performs a matching search using supported identifiers such as:

* VIN,
* engine number,
* or other supported identifiers.

Plate numbers are not treated as primary trusted identifiers because vehicle plates may change in Ghana.

### Public Disclosure Rules

Only limited information relating to verified stolen or recovered vehicles may be displayed publicly.

Pending, rejected, or non-public cases are not publicly disclosed.

---

## 6. Contact Sharing and Recovery Communications

Owner contact details are not automatically published publicly.

Where a vehicle owner explicitly opts in, communication may occur through a controlled “Reveal Contact” workflow or protected recovery interaction system.

This process is designed to:

* reduce fraud,
* prevent scraping,
* support auditability,
* monitor abuse,
* and protect affected vehicle owners.

## 12. Data Retention

We retain data only as long as reasonably necessary for operational, legal, audit, fraud prevention, and recovery purposes.

Current retention guidelines include:

| Data Type             | Retention                        |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Rejected cases        | 6 months                         |
| Recovered cases       | 1 year                           |
| Verified stolen cases | Until recovery                   |
| Police documents      | Same duration as associated case |
| Audit logs            | 7 years                          |
| Sighting reports      | Until recovery                   |

Certain records may be retained longer where legally required or necessary for fraud investigations or dispute resolution.
